MELBOURNE
MONOLITH

MELBOURNE MONOLITH is a bespoke display typeface inspired by the brutalist architectural rigour of the city's concrete giants. It is an exploration of mass and void, where negative space is as deliberate as the strokes themselves. Designed for maximum impact in editorial and environmental contexts.

Melbourne Monolith is more than a typeface; it is a structural observation. This specimen captures the brutalist rigour of the foundry's philosophy, translated onto 180gsm uncoated architectural paper. Every stroke is designed to command the page with the weight of concrete.

The Humble Brief

A rigorous exploration into the materiality of digital forms. The Melbourne Monolith typeface was upsampled and re-evaluated for analogue application, specifically investigating how ink absorption on high-grammage paper interacts with geometric precision.

The specimen serves as a physical anchor for a digital-first identity. It is a monolith in the sense of its presence—unwavering, brutalist, yet deeply rooted in the editorial traditions of the Victorian design landscape.

SIGNAGE APPLICATION:
MELBOURNE MONOLITH

An inquiry into the threshold between two-dimensional graphic rigour and three-dimensional architectural materiality.

Scale & Materiality

The Humble Brief

The transition of the Melbourne Monolith typeface from analogue paper sketches to the built environment required a fundamental shift in technical rigour.

Born from the brutalist landscape of Melbourne’s civic core, the typeface demands a physical presence that matches its geometric weight. The project explores how ink-traps translate into light-traps when etched into 316 Stainless Steel or cast into high-density concrete.
